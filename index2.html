The Clark Amish Community

The Clark Amish community was settled in 1953 and is currently one of Missouri's largest Amish settlements, alongside the Seymour and Jamesport settlements. There are over 1,530 people spread out over more than 15 square miles in Audrain, Randolph & Monroe counties with more than 50 family operated businesses.

The community was founded primarily by Iowa Amish seeking greater freedom after conflicts over education in the state.  The Global Anabaptist Mennonite Encyclopedia Online describes the Amish families of the Clark settlement living “in a tightly knit area with Amish farms adjacent to each other for miles along its country roads”.

The Clark Amish is an Old Order Amish settlement.
